Understanding Agriculture in India
Traditional farming practices meeting modern techniques

Agriculture has played a central role in India's history, economy, and culture. It provides livelihoods to over half the population and contributes significantly to the GDP. Major reforms like the Green Revolution and modern policies aim to balance production with sustainability.

Today, Indian agriculture stands at a crossroads, embracing technology while preserving traditional wisdom. The sector is evolving with precision farming, digital marketplaces, and climate-smart practices to ensure food security for the nation.

Important Note

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is highly encouraged as a sustainable approach that combines biological, cultural, physical and chemical tools to minimize economic, health, and environmental risks.

Current Trends in Indian Agriculture
  • Precision Agriculture: Utilization of GPS, drones, and IoT devices for optimized farming.
  • Digital Advisory: Mobile apps providing real-time weather, market prices, and expert advice.
  • eNAM Platform: National Agriculture Market connecting farmers directly to buyers across India.
  • Water Conservation: Adoption of micro-irrigation, drip systems, and rainwater harvesting.
  • Organic Farming: Growing demand leading to increased certified organic cultivation.
