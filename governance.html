Overview of Indian Governance
The Parliament of India - House of Democracy

Governance in India is built on democratic principles, ensuring justice, equality, and transparency. The government operates through a structured system of legislative, executive, and judicial branches.

Structure of Governance
Supreme Court of India - Guardian of the Constitution
  • Legislative: Parliament (Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha) creates laws.
  • Executive: The President, Prime Minister, and ministries enforce policies.
  • Judiciary: An independent body upholding constitutional values.
  • Constitutional Bodies: Election Commission, CAG, UPSC etc.
  • Three-Tier System: Central, State, and Local governance.
Administrative Reforms & E-Governance

With the advent of technology, India is embracing digital governance. Initiatives like Digital India and e-Governance Services aim to deliver services efficiently, transparently, and inclusively.

Digital Transformation

India ranks 3rd globally in digital government services adoption, with over 1.2 billion Aadhaar enrollments and 300+ services available on UMANG app.

Key Governance Initiatives
  • RTI Act (2005): Right to Information empowers citizens to access government records.
  • Digital India (2015): Digital infrastructure for governance and services.
  • MyGov (2014): Citizen engagement platform for policy discussions.
  • UMANG (2017): Unified Mobile App for 1700+ e-Gov services.
  • CPGRAMS (2007): Centralized Public Grievance Redressal system.
Citizen Participation
Voting - The Foundation of Democratic Participation

Citizens are at the heart of Indian democracy. Platforms like MyGov and the RTI Act empower people to be informed and involved in governance processes.
