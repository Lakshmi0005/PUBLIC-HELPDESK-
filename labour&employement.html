Labour and Employment in India

Overview

The Ministry of Labour & Employment is one of the oldest and most important ministries of the Government of India. It aims to enhance the quality of life of workers by providing social security, ensuring workplace safety, and creating better employment opportunities.

India's workforce is undergoing a transformation with digital initiatives and policy reforms to create a more inclusive and productive labor market.

Key Focus Areas

  • Labour Laws Simplification and Codification: Streamlining complex regulations into comprehensive codes
  • Welfare and Social Security for Workers: Expanding coverage to unorganized sector workers
  • Employment Services and Career Counseling: Connecting job seekers with opportunities nationwide
  • Skill Development and Apprenticeships: Bridging the gap between education and employment
  • Occupational Health and Workplace Safety: Ensuring safe working conditions across industries

Digital and Policy Initiatives

The government has launched several digital platforms like the Shram Suvidha Portal, National Career Service (NCS), and e-SHRAM for improved governance and access to services. These platforms help in reducing paperwork, ensuring compliance, and facilitating employment.

Labour Codes

To streamline labor laws, the government has introduced four Labour Codes:

  • Code on Wages: Standardizing wage payments across sectors
  • Industrial Relations Code: Modernizing employer-employee relations
  • Social Security Code: Expanding welfare coverage
  • Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code: Ensuring workplace safety standards

Skill Development and Employment

Through programs like the National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) and Skill India Mission, youth are being trained for jobs across sectors, boosting both employability and economic growth.

What's New

  • Launch of e-SHRAM portal for unorganised sector workers
  • Revised safety standards for hazardous industries
  • Online registration for the PM Shram Yogi Maandhan Scheme
  • New labor code implementation guidelines
  • Expansion of apprenticeship opportunities

Did You Know?

India has over 500 million workforce – the second largest in the world.

  • e-SHRAM aims to register 38 crore unorganised workers.
  • NCS lists over 1 crore jobs and career resources.
  • Apprenticeships have grown by 40% in last 3 years.
  • 80% of India's workforce is in the informal sector.
